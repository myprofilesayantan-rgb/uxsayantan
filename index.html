Case Study

DESIGN FOR HEALTHCARE ECOSYSTEM

Elders need freedom over dependency.
A silent care and self guided system can only
bring back the trust for them. The trust they
have lost from online care systems.

Elder Voice now

I am not a kid. They worry constantly when I go out, calling me all through the day.

Caregiver Voice now

My mother went out for three hours and didn't call even once. I was honestly starting to panic.

Elder Voice now

I never forget my medicine. But Sometime skip or take late. That is fine.

Caregiver Voice now

When it comes to her medicines, forget it. I have to keep reminding him throughout the day.

Elder Voice now

Sometime I forget the road and it happened 2 to 3 times. Rescued by my family.

Caregiver Voice now

I tried calling my father, but he was busy playing cards on his phone and rejected my call. So I had no idea how my mother was doing. She has no phone.

Elder Voice now

Avoid messaging app, So much noise with notification.

Caregiver Voice now

I don't know why he's like this sometimes. Even when she's unwell, he rarely calls or gives an update.

Elder Voice now

Feel helpless when I am home alone. My son in US. He calls everyday. I can feel what they think :)

Conducted using three complementary methods—interviewing during the activity, debriefing after the activity, and silent observation. Captures decisions in the moment, not reconstructed memory.

Affinity Mapping

Raw observations clustered into behavioral patterns. Reveals what's actually happening beneath stated preferences and post-hoc rationalizations.

Confident but scared Surveillance over independence Careful but quietly careless Dignity over dependency Trust, slowly withdrawn from life
THE SYSTEM

So we designed for this specific tension.

We reframed the problem around the family, not just the individual. Instead of daily, anxious check-in calls, Tracto communicates through passive coordinates.

A single, ambient status indicator tells the caregiver "All is well" without demanding active input from the elder, preserving their independence.
